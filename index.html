About Trevor Judd

Background

I am a veteran of the United States Air Force and a 2020 graduate of Tennessee Tech University's Computer Science program. Currently, I work as a software engineer at Dynetics supporting Redstone Arsenal, where I apply my skills in test automation and software development to deliver high-quality solutions.

Military Service

I served in the Air Force from 2009 to 2015 as a nuclear ICBM Mechanical Maintenance Technician, working on the Minuteman III weapon system. I was stationed at F.E. Warren AFB in Cheyenne, Wyoming, and was awarded the Air Force Achievement Medal for meritorious service. This experience taught me discipline, attention to detail, and the ability to work under pressure.

Journey & Locations

Originally from Cookeville, Tennessee, my journey has taken me across the country and around the world. After my military service ended in 2015, I lived in Fort Collins, Colorado for two years before returning to my hometown to pursue my education and career in software engineering. I currently reside in Elkmont, Alabama, and work in Huntsville.
